The Problem 02 / 10
A Regulatory Clock Is Ticking.
Most Banks Are Not Ready.
RBI's January 2026 IET mandate fundamentally changed the risk calculus of cheque processing. The industry has yet to fully reckon with what that means.
What Changed
3 Hours
IET window per inward cheque (Item Expiry Time)

RBI now mandates that every inward cheque must receive a clearing decision within the Item Expiry Time (IET). Miss the window — regardless of reason — and the instrument is deemed approved by the presenting bank. The drawee bank pays. No exceptions.

A missed IET is not a process failure. It is a financial liability. The bank absorbs the loss even if the cheque was fraudulent.
The Compound Risk
⏱

Volume × Velocity = Exposure

A bank processing 5,000 inward cheques per day at 3-hour IET has 15,000+ active deadlines running concurrently at peak. A single batch delay cascades into mass deemed approvals.

⚠

Human Review Is the Bottleneck

Fraud suspected → human queue → wait → IET breach. The very safety mechanism that should protect banks becomes the source of regulatory risk.

The Systemic Gap
BEFORE Jan 2026 High Risk
📥 Cheque received → Manual queue → Fraud screening (unbounded time) → Decision
Timing risk: largely unconstrained. Delays absorbed silently.
AFTER Jan 2026 Zero Tolerance
📥 Cheque received → 3-hour absolute deadline starts immediately
Miss → Deemed approval → Bank pays regardless of fraud status
Every delay has a direct P&L consequence.

The IET Safety Net

An intelligent watchdog monitors every in-flight cheque. At a defined threshold before IET expiry, it automatically files a protective decision — even if human review is still pending. No cheque can fall through.

When 500 cheques arrive in one batch, they are all processed simultaneously. The bank's clearing window is measured in seconds — not the regulatory 3 hours.
